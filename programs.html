Our Programs


The Regents Scholar Society hosts several different programs that involve different members of the UCLA community. We host events for the general UCLA public - like our speaker event SPIEL, along with programs that give back to our local Westwood community - like our Los Angeles Mentorship Program. We also have RSS-specific programs, like our Faculty Mentorship Program, where members of the society are paired with esteemed members of the faculty. Learn more about the various programs that RSS at UCLA using the links!

SPIEL 2018


SPIEL stands for Students Presenting, Innovating, Entertaining and Learning. Inspired by TED Talks, this campus-wide speaker event is put on by students, for students. The aim of SPIEL is to give students from all backgrounds the chance to talk about something that is not only important to the presenters themselves, but is also often important to the greater UCLA and global community. In the past, SPIEL presenters have addressed a variety of topics, ranging from mental health to ground-breaking student research.

Come watch SPIEL on April 4th, 2018 from 6-8PM in the Ackerman Viewpoint Room!

Faculty Mentorship Program


Every year, faculty members from across the campus get involved with the Regents Scholar Society through our Faculty Mentorship Program. This program matches current Regent Scholars to an esteemed faculty member - giving RSSers the exclusive opportunity to form a connection to faculty. In the past, program participants have established strong relationships with their mentor, even to the point where the faculty member became one of their letters of recommendation.

Los Angeles Mentorship Program


The Los Angeles Mentorship Program (LAMP) provides UCLA students the opportunity to give back to the local community through tutoring at University High School. This program aims to provide resources to underserved youth - giving them access to SAT and ACT preparation along with workshops regarding financial literacy, college applications, and more.

Students interested in tutoring and volunteering through LAMP are not required nor expected to have previous experience, although strong interpersonal and communication skills are required to succeed in the role.

If you'd like to learn more about becoming a LAMP tutor, please contact lamp@rssla.org for more information. You can also redirect any questions you have about the program to that email. Thank you for your interest!

Overnight Stay Program


The Regents Scholar Society hosts 4 Overnight Stay Program sessions to allow prospective students to come get to know the UCLA campus! You get to tour our campus, see our housing options, taste our food, and be a true bruin for a weekend! This overnight program is a must for those who are debating between schools - come to UCLA and fall in love with the campus like we did!

At OSP, expect that...

  • You'll live in the residence halls with your host, experiencing dorm life and eating at UCLA's dining halls - which are ranked #1 in the country!
  • You get to explore Westwood, UCLA's attached college town, and get a taste of life near movie premieres, delicious food, and museum culture!
  • You'll be taken on campus tours, getting to see the residence halls, Royce Hall, Powell Library, our Sculpture Gardens, our world-class libraries, and more!
  • You get to shadow a class, and see what college academia is truly like!
  • You'll make friends - regardless of if you decide to matriculate or not!

Interested in attending? Check out our prospective scholars site here for more information on how to sign up! Have questions? Feel free to email our Outreach Director here.
